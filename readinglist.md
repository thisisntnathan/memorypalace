## 27 Feb 2025  

- [Chemiluminescent 2-Coumaranones: Synthesis, Luminescence Mechanism, and Emerging Applications](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-j7zl0?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
Stefan Schramm, Tim Lippold, Isabelle Navizet  
*Organic Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-26  
&ensp;The paper reviews 2-Coumaranones, promising chemiluminescent compounds known for efficient light emission. It covers their synthesis, structural properties, and mechanisms, highlighting applications in bioanalytical and material sciences such as enzyme-catalyzed assays and mechanoluminescence. Challenges include optimizing quantum yield and solvent compatibility. Future research aims to develop tunable derivatives for enhanced applications in diagnostics and sensing.  

- [Enhancing Uncertainty Quantification in Molecular Machine Learning: A Comparative Study of Deep Evidential Regression and Ensembles with Posthoc Calibration](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-fx019?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
BIDHAN CHANDRA GARAIN, Matheus O. Bispo, Max Pinheiro, Jr., Mario Barbatti  
*Theoretical and Computational Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-26  
&ensp;The paper evaluates uncertainty quantification (UQ) in molecular property prediction, using an enhanced equivariant graph neural network (EGNN-DER) and an ensemble of ANI models. It addresses underconfident uncertainties with posthoc calibration methods and highlights the effectiveness of adversarial group calibration in improving active learning. The findings emphasize the need for calibration in refining machine learning strategies in computational chemistry.  

- [Development of Parallel On-the-Fly Crystal Algorithm for Reaction Discovery in Large and Complex Molecular Systems](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-9hcdq-v2?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
Ruibin Liang, Ankit Pandey, Gustavo J. Costa, Alam Mushfiq, Bill Poirier  
*Theoretical and Computational Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-26  
&ensp;The paper presents a parallel on-the-fly Crystal algorithm optimized for reaction discovery in large molecular systems, specifically applied to bilirubin and novel molecular photoswitches. Using a QM/MM approach, it explores configurational spaces to uncover new product minima and reaction pathways, showcasing the algorithm's efficacy in enhancing understanding of chemical reactivities.  

- [Predicting the binding sites of alkali atoms on transition metal dichalcogenides with a machine learning model based on electrostatics](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-l3gpw?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
Raheel Hammad, Soumya Ghosh  
*Theoretical and Computational Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-26  
&ensp;The paper presents a machine learning model that predicts alkali atom binding sites on transition metal dichalcogenides (TMDs) using electrostatic energies. It highlights challenges with traditional DFT methods and shows limited correlation with point charge analysis. The model accurately ranks binding site energies for various alkali ion concentrations and TMD types, enhancing predictions for battery and optoelectronic applications.  

- [Frontier Orbital Changes Induce Slow-down of Excited State Population Dynamics in Thiopyridone Isomers](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-cjls7?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
Sambit Kumar Das, Garratt Douglas, Gaffney Kelly, Michael Odelius  
*Theoretical and Computational Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-26  
&ensp;The study investigates the non-adiabatic dynamics of photoexcited thiopyridone isomers (ortho, meta, para) using TDDFT-based surface hopping dynamics. The research reveals a systematic decrease in intersystem crossing rates from ortho to para isomer and underscores the role of electronic structure in these dynamics. Comparisons with a multireference approach enhance the understanding of excited-state proton transfer processes.  

- [Meta effect enables redder and larger Stokes shift chromophores by enhanced aromaticity reversal](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-56x1p?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
Chong Fang, Cheng Chen, Jiawei Liu, Ivan N. Myasnyanko, Daniil I. Rudik, Anita H. Adams, Alice R. Walker, Mikhail S. Baranov  
*Theoretical and Computational Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-26  
&ensp;The paper investigates the "meta effect" in compact fluorescent molecules with meta-positioned donor and acceptor groups. It finds that meta chromophores exhibit redder emissions and larger Stokes shifts compared to para isomers due to enhanced aromaticity reversal. The study proposes methods to improve fluorescence quantum yields while utilizing large Stokes shifts for applications in bioimaging and optoelectronics.

## 26 Feb 2025  

- [Development of simplified, photo-active, and TfR1-targeted poly(β-aminoester)-zwitterion nanovehicles for gene delivery across brain endothelium](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-wcxb8?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
Benjamín Oller-Salvia, Maria C. Lucana, Shambhavi Pandey, Salvador Borrós  
*Biological and Medicinal Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-25  
&ensp;The study presents a simplified, photo-active poly(β-aminoester) formulation (pBAE-CRHR-Zw) for targeted gene delivery across the blood-brain barrier. By incorporating a zwitterionic moiety and a peptide targeting TfR1, the improved nanovehicles demonstrate enhanced transfection and cellular internalization in a human cell model. The findings highlight the potential for effective gene therapy strategies.  

- [Bicyclic selenenyl sulfides tune stepwise rates of thiol/selenol addition, resolution, and cyclisation, to reveal cellular constraints for bioreductive probes targeting mammalian thioredoxin reductase](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-6vbn2?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
Oliver Thorn-Seshold, Lukas Zeisel, Lucas Dessen-Weissenhorn, Karoline C. Scholzen, Andrea Madabeni, Laura Orian, Elias S. J. Arnér  
*Biological and Medicinal Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-25  
&ensp;The paper explores how bicyclic selenenyl sulfides (SeSP) are engineered to regulate reaction rates relevant to the mammalian thioredoxin reductase (TrxR1). Through a five-step synthesis and profiling various isomers, the study demonstrates that altering reaction kinetics can enhance cellular selectivity, revealing potential for using multistep probes in biological systems.  

- [Biocompatible photochemistry for the spatiotemporal control of microbial growth](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-qdjjg?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
Stephen Wallace, Thomas Thorpe, Natasha Kisseroudis  
*Biological and Medicinal Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-25  
&ensp;The paper presents a novel biocompatible photochemical method to achieve spatiotemporal control of microbial growth by utilizing a light-responsive o-nitrobenzyl-caged metabolite. This technique successfully regulates auxotrophic Escherichia coli growth in liquid cultures and co-cultures, enhancing precision in microbial engineering without invasiveness, thus broadening the potential of synthetic biology applications.  

- [It Matters Where the Heavy Atom is Placed: Optimizing the Spin-Orbital Coupling in Multiresonant TADF (MR-TADF) Emitters and Its Impact on OLED Performance](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-m13j8?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
Eli Zysman-Colman, Dongyang Chen, Hui Wang, Feng Huang, David Cordes, Aidan McKay, Kai Wang, Xiao-Hong Zhang  
*Organic Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-25  
&ensp;This paper investigates how the placement of heavy chalcogen atoms affects the properties of multiresonant thermally activated delayed fluorescence (MR-TADF) emitters. Two pairs of isomeric compounds were synthesized. The findings show that specific isomers (tDPABT2B and tCzBT2B) exhibit higher spin-orbital coupling and better OLED performance, achieving maximum external quantum efficiencies around 34%.  

- [Predicting the stability of base-mediated C–H carboxylation adducts using data science tools](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-5tlrl?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
Maike Eckhoff, Aleria Garcia-Roca, Shubham Deolka, Liudmila Seidel, Matthew S. Sigman, Jonny Proppe  
*Organic Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-25  
&ensp;The paper presents a predictive workflow integrating quantum chemistry and statistical modeling to assess the stability of base-mediated C–H carboxylation adducts. It analyzes 60 novel carbon-centered nucleophiles' CO2 affinities. Experimental validation on five predicted nucleophiles confirms three stable and two unstable adducts, showcasing the method's efficacy in organic synthesis using CO2.  

- [Single molecule peptide synthesis without any side-chain protection using a nanopore and a sub-nanometer positioning system](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-gsw8x-v2?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
G Sampath  
*Organic Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-25  
&ensp;The paper presents a novel approach for single molecule peptide synthesis without side-chain protection using a nanopore and precision positioning system. The method involves coupling amino acids in cycles while preventing aggregation, with minimal reagent use and reduced cleanup. High capacities and extensive peptide lengths are feasible, indicating a sustainable synthesis technique.  

- [MolGen-Transformer: A molecule language model for the generation and latent space exploration of pi-conjugated molecules](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-n0tnl?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
Chad Risko, Chih-Hsuan Yang, Rebekah Duke, Parker Sornberger, Moses Ogbaje, Baskar Ganapathysubramanian  
*Theoretical and Computational Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-25  
&ensp;The MolGen-Transformer is a generative AI model for designing pi-conjugated molecules, trained on 198 million organic molecules. It achieves 100% reconstruction accuracy using the SELFIES representation. The model enables diverse molecule generation, chemically similar molecule creation, and latent space interpolation. The model and methods are publicly available, enhancing AI-driven molecular discovery.  

- [Ternary Complex Modeling, Induced Fit Docking and Molecular Dynamics Simulations as a Successful Approach for the Design of VHL-mediated PROTACs Targeting the Kinase FLT3](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-2n40d-v2?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
Wolfgang Sippl, Husam Nassar, Anne-Christin Sarnow, Ismail Celik, Mohamed Abdelsalam, Dina Robaa  
*Theoretical and Computational Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-25  
&ensp;The paper discusses a computational approach combining ternary complex modeling, induced fit docking, and molecular dynamics simulations for designing VHL-mediated PROTACs targeting FLT3. The methodology was validated using four ternary complex structures and demonstrated predictive power for 3D structures, with MD simulations confirming complex stability and highlighting the role of structural dynamics in PROTAC design.  

- [QC-Augmented GNNs for Few-Shot Prediction of Amorphous Polymer Properties via Multi-Scale Microstructures](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-nt0jx?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
Yiwen Zhang, Zihao YE, Dejun HU, Shutao QI, Zuobang SUN, Junfeng YANG, Yan MA, Yi ZHANG, Junliang ZHANG, Zhiming LI  
*Theoretical and Computational Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-25  
&ensp;The paper introduces Locluster, a predictive model combining quantum chemistry-derived descriptors with graph convolutional networks for predicting properties of amorphous polymers. It efficiently identifies critical properties using only 2-5 descriptors and approximately 20 training samples, achieving results comparable to traditional methods trained on larger datasets. This approach addresses the challenges of data scarcity in polymer design.  

- [Open-Source Generation of Sigma Profiles: Impact of Quantum Chemistry and Solvation Treatment on Machine Learning Performance](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-jggvw?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
Fathya Salih, Dinis Abranches, Edward Maginn, Yamil Colón  
*Theoretical and Computational Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-25  
&ensp;The paper discusses the development of open-source software for generating sigma profiles—a molecular descriptor combining physical significance with low dimensionality. It explores the impact of quantum chemistry and solvation treatment on model performance using these profiles in Gaussian Process models. Findings indicate increased theoretical levels do not enhance prediction accuracy. Recommendations for sigma profile usage are also provided.  

- [Elucidating Pt/C and ionomer aggregation dynamics in the manufacturing of Fuel Cell catalyst layers: a discrete element method approach](https://dx.doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-t5rxn?rft_dat=source%3Ddrss)  
Alejandro A. Franco, Matthias Baldofski, Sourab Barath Vijayaraghavan  
*Theoretical and Computational Chemistry on ChemRxiv*  
2025-02-25  
&ensp;The paper presents a mesoscale modeling workflow using the Discrete Element Method (DEM) to analyze Pt/C and ionomer aggregation in fuel cell catalyst layers. It investigates how manufacturing parameters, particularly solvent composition, impact ionomer aggregation, finding that lower water content reduces secondary aggregation. Various micro-structural descriptors were used to study catalyst-ionomer distributions.
